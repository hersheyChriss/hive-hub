This is the story of the Time Cards

Legend has it that there was once a fellow by the name of James that forgot to submit and approve his timecards once.
The following Monday, he went about his day the usual way, starting the morning off with a cup of coffee and walking to his office with a smile on his face.
He walked into his room only to find there was no chair. Looking closer, there was a chair…but it had been shrunk down to the size of a mouse!
James was completely baffled, so he walked over to his mentor’s office to see if he could get some answers there.
The door was closed, lights were off, and his mentor was nowhere to be found. Disappointed, he ventured back to his office.
As he walked in, he turned quickly to notice that this time his door was missing!
“Was that gone earlier too?” he thought.
He was starting to get spooked, but he knew he had responsibilities.
So, he walked over to his desk, awkwardly stood hunched over his keyboard and house, and logged in.
James opened Outlook for his morning email check, and noticed he had quite a few more emails than normal.
As he read through them, his heart sunk.
“We have not received an approved timecard from you Mr. James”, “Mr. James, we are still waiting on an approved timecard”, “This is your final warning Mr. James, we must have an approved timecard or further actions will be taken.”
He hovered his mouse over the final email, sent early this morning. “You’re dead kiddo.” was all it read.
James quickly locked his computer(because he doesn’t want to get baggy pants’d) and ran out of his office into the hallway.
He looked into every office, desperately trying to find another soul in the office, but every door was wide open with no one in it.
He got to the elevators, but the call button did not light up and no elevator opened. He pressed every button, still nothing.
He rushed to the stairs, but they were also locked.
He looked around in confusion, hoping to see a bunch of people pop up, shouting out that it was all just a prank.
But it was dead silent.
He stood still for a few seconds, hearing nothing but his heavy breathing.
He needed to calm down.
He walked slowly back to his office.
The choices were clear.
He walked into his office, hunched over his keyboard and mouse again, unlocked his computer, and opened up iportal.
He navigated to the employee portal, and from there he accessed eTime.
He filled out his hours, and he saved them and the pressed Approve.
He watched as the loading bar circled and circled, seemingly forever. But then, after what felt like a lifetime, the words across the screen read, “Your timecard has been approved!”
A sigh of relief fell over him, and he fell back.
But he fell into his chair. It was full sized again! And his door was back! He ran out into the hallway, and everyone was in their offices just like normal!
The day had been saved! It was some horrid nightmare!
Or…was it real?
He would never know.
But one thing was certain.
James would never forget to approve his timecard again.
